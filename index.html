Networking Stuff

I documented all of our networking on the managed switches out of necessity, and discovered that our 48 port ITL switch was about to die. At a meeting, we decided to buy one new 48 port switch so that the ITL switch can be replaced, and was replaced without issue.

When we changed over to the new 48 port switch, some things changed - we removed SW1 from between Ziltoid and SWM1, and removed another ITL switch entirely (which was being used for backup networking as the old switch died)

Ziltoid was implemented for the servers by Bobby, and I helped configure the managed switches with STP for live-failover so that when one link to Ziltoid dies, another shall take its place, and if Ziltoid dies, we can still communicate between SWM2 and SWM3.

I also configured what was SW4 and thus SWM4 came to be. It's 10 years old, but it will continue to serve COSI for the near future.

Hydra

Hydra is our new 32 core, 64GB RAM VM host.
My job, along with Baha, was to put together this machine and to install the software. Baha specced out the hardware late last semester, and the parts arrived over the summer. On the first weekend, we created Hydra. Baha assembled the parts, and then I installed the software, backed up the VM's, and then did the transition from Felix to Hydra. I handed off Felix to Bobby, who will now be running Androbattery natively on hardware, since it really put a toll on Felix as a VM, eating most of the ram and all of the CPU much of the time, and the image would not resize properly without some difficult tweaking.

It runs about 10 services currently, including a hodge podge of pretty much every service COSI has to offer.
